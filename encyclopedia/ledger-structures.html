How It Works

Ledger structures function through the principles of dual-balance and cost minimization. Each recognition event splits into potential and realized halves, creating cycles that must be managed without overlap. This management ensures that the total cost associated with these events remains low, adhering to the overarching principle of efficiency in the universe.

Key Properties

  • Dual-Balance: Every transaction must have a corresponding counterpart, ensuring symmetry in the ledger.
  • Cost Minimization: The structure is designed to minimize the total cost of recognition events, leading to efficient interactions.
