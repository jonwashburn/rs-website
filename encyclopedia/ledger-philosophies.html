How It Works

Ledger philosophies operate on several foundational principles:

  • Countability: All transactions must be discrete and quantifiable.
  • Dual-Balance: Every cost incurred must be balanced by a corresponding recognition.
  • Cost Minimization: The system seeks to minimize the total cost of transactions.

These principles work together to ensure that the ledger remains consistent and self-sustaining, allowing for a coherent representation of reality.
