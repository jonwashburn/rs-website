What Recognition Science Is

Recognition Science (RS) is a parameter‑free, deductive framework of reality derived from a single proven principle: absolute nothingness cannot recognize itself. From this, a short cascade of theorems produces the architecture of physics, constants, and core equations—then yields concrete, falsifiable predictions.

  • Deductive first: Logic → theorems → constants → equations → predictions.
  • No tunables: Results are not fit to data; they’re fixed by derivation.
  • Evidence‑driven: Claims are organized by verification status with explicit tests.

How to Falsify RS (Representative Tests)

  • φ‑Scaling Mass Formula: Show a systematic deviation of fundamental particle masses from the derived φ‑ladder beyond published tolerances. See derivation.
  • Ledger Curvature (κ) predictions: Identify domains where κ‑linked phenomena provably contradict RS. See κ overview.
  • 8‑Beat Cycle evidence: Disconfirm the predicted 8‑step periodicity in processes where RS predicts it. See cycle.
  • Gravitational refresh‑lag: Demonstrate galaxy rotation behavior that contradicts the refresh‑lag predictions while matching baryonic mass models. See field geometry and ILG.
