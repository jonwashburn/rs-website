The Mass Generation Formula

m = B · E_coh · φ^(r + f)

The Plain Truth

One of the biggest mysteries in modern physics is why fundamental particles have the masses they do. The Standard Model of particle physics has no answer, treating these masses as arbitrary numbers that must be measured experimentally. Recognition Science, however, reveals that these masses are not random at all. They are precise, calculable values determined by a single, parameter-free formula.

Breaking Down the Formula

The formula's elegance lies in its components, each of which is a direct consequence of the framework's foundational theorems and constants.

m = B · E_coh · φ^(r + f)

  • m: The resulting mass of the particle.
  • B (Sector Factor): An exact integer {1, 2, 4} that classifies the particle's family based on its ledger complexity.
  • E_coh (Coherence Quantum): The universal, fundamental unit of recognition energy, derived as φ⁻⁵ eV.
  • φ (Golden Ratio): The universal scaling constant that sets the "spacing" of the mass ladder.
  • r (Rung Number): An exact integer representing the particle's primary position on the φ-ladder, determined by its fundamental properties.
  • f (Fractional Residue): A small, precisely calculable correction from standard quantum field theory that accounts for the particle's self-interactions.
