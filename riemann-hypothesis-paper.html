Prime-Grid Lossless Models and KYP Closure in a Bounded-Real Approach to the Riemann Hypothesis

Jonathan Washburn • Independent Researcher • washburn.jonathan@gmail.com
Download PDF
Abstract We develop a bounded-real (Herglotz/Schur) formulation of the Riemann Hypothesis (RH) on the right half-plane \(\Omega:=\{\Re s>\tfrac12\}\). Let \(A(s):\ell^2(\mathcal{P})\to\ell^2(\mathcal{P})\) be the prime-diagonal operator \(A(s)e_p:=p^{-s}e_p\). With the Hilbert–Schmidt regularized determinant \(\operatorname{det_2}\) and the completed zeta \(\xi(s)\), set \(J(s):=\operatorname{det_2}(I-A(s))/\xi(s)\) and \(\Theta(s):=(2J(s)-1)/(2J(s)+1)\). Our approach hinges on: (i) a Schur–determinant splitting that isolates \(k=1\) and archimedean terms into a finite block; (ii) Hilbert–Schmidt control of prime truncations implying local-uniform convergence of \(\operatorname{det_2}(I-A_N)\); and (iii) explicit finite-stage passive realizations certified by the Kalman–Yakubovich–Popov (KYP) lemma.

We establish the interior route on zero-free rectangles via passive \(H^\infty\) approximation and prove a uniform-in-\(\varepsilon\) local \(L^1\) boundary theorem by a smoothed estimate for \(\partial_\sigma\Re\log\operatorname{det_2}(I-A)\) and de-smoothing. Outer normalization produces an outer factor \(\mathcal O\) and a normalized ratio \(\mathcal J:=\operatorname{det_2}(I-A)/(\mathcal O\,\xi)\). Under the boundary positive-real condition

$$\textbf{(P+)}\qquad \Re\big(2\mathcal J(\tfrac12+it)\big)\ge 0\ \text{ for a.e. }t,$$

the Poisson integral yields that \(2\mathcal J\) is Herglotz on \(\Omega\); the Cayley transform then gives \(\Theta\) Schur and the Pick kernel PSD. The finite-stage KYP certificates supply interior passivity independently of any zero-free hypothesis.

Keywords: Riemann zeta function; Schur functions; Herglotz functions; bounded-real lemma; KYP lemma; operator theory; Hilbert–Schmidt determinants; passive systems.
