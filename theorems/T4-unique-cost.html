Theorem 4: Uniqueness of the Cost Functional

J(x) = ½(x + 1/x) under symmetry, finiteness, and normalization

Statement

The only analytic, dual‑balanced, ledger‑finite cost functional on ℝ>0 is J(x) = ½(x + 1/x).

Sketch

  • Symmetric Laurent expansion from J(x) = J(1/x).
  • Growth bound kills higher |n| ≥ 2 modes.
  • Normalization and positivity fix the ½ factor.

Implications

  • Provides the convex cost used in prediction and interference estimates.
  • Anchors later constructions (e.g., daily score cost on the Astrology OS).
