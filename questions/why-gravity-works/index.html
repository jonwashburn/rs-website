The Problem That Has Stumped Physics

Gravity is simultaneously the most familiar and most mysterious force in the universe. We live with it every moment of our lives, yet despite centuries of scientific progress, we have no idea why it exists.

Einstein's General Relativity describes gravity with exquisite mathematical precision—it can predict the motion of planets, the bending of light around stars, and the existence of gravitational waves. But it doesn't explain why mass and energy curve spacetime in the first place. It simply states that they do, leaving the fundamental question unanswered.

Newton was even more honest about this mystery. When asked to explain the cause of gravity, he famously replied "I do not make hypotheses." He could describe how gravity worked mathematically, but freely admitted he had no idea what caused it.

Understanding the fundamental mystery of gravity

Yet we remain completely ignorant about the underlying mechanism that makes any of it possible.
