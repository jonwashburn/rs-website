/// SECTION 1 ///
THE CRISIS OF FREE PARAMETERS

Modern physics is a miracle of description, yet it rests on a fractured foundation. The Standard Model of particle physics and the \(\Lambda\)CDM model of cosmology are the two most successful theories ever created, but they are incomplete. To function, they require over twenty "free parameters"—fundamental constants (like the mass of an electron) that are plugged in by hand because the theories cannot predict them.

This reliance on external inputs is a clear signal that our current understanding is a sophisticated description of reality, not a fundamental explanation. A true theory of everything should not be "tuned" to match the universe; it should derive the universe, parameters and all, from first principles. This is the crisis Recognition Science was born to solve.
/// SECTION 2 ///
THE SINGLE POINT OF TRUTH

Recognition Science begins not with a physical postulate, but with a single, self-evident logical tautology: the impossibility of self-referential non-existence.

Stated simply: for "nothing" to exist, it would need the property of "being nothing." But having a property is a form of existence. This creates a paradox. Absolute nothingness cannot recognize its own state without ceasing to be nothing. This single, inescapable truth—the Meta-Principle—is the logical engine that forces reality to exist. It is the one solid piece of ground from which the entire universe can be, and must be, built.
