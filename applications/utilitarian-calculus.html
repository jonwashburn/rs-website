Utilitarian Calculus

A Special Case of Universal Curvature Minimization

The Physics of Utilitarianism

In Recognition Science, the principles of utilitarianism—seeking the "greatest good for the greatest number"—emerge not as a philosophical choice, but as a direct consequence of the physics of ledger curvature. Utilitarian calculus is a simplified case of the Universal Moral Law, focusing on the aggregate state of a system rather than the intricacies of individual interactions.

The Mathematical Definition

Utilitarianism is the ethical framework that seeks to minimize total positive curvature (suffering) and maximize the number of states at or near zero curvature (well-being) across a system.

For a system of $N$ moral states, the utilitarian ideal is to select an action $A$ that:

1. Minimizes total suffering: $\min \sum_{i=1}^{N} \max(\kappa(A(s_i)), 0)$

2. Maximizes states at balance: $\max |\{s_i : \kappa(A(s_i)) = 0\}|$

This provides a rigorous, quantitative foundation for utilitarian principles, removing the ambiguity of terms like "happiness" or "utility" and replacing them with the precise, measurable quantity of ledger curvature.

Properties of Utilitarian Calculus

Provides a Quantitative Framework

Recognition Science provides a concrete metric (curvature) for utilitarian calculations, allowing for objective, data-driven ethical analysis rather than subjective philosophical debate.

Resolves Utilitarian Paradoxes

Classic utilitarian paradoxes (like the "utility monster") are resolved because individual coherence acts as a constraint. An action that maximizes total utility at the cost of decohering (destroying) a single moral state is mathematically disproven, as it removes a participant from the ledger entirely, which is an infinite-cost event.

Integrates Rights and Duties

Individual rights are not external constraints but emerge as necessary conditions for maintaining a stable, low-curvature system. The "right" to not be harmed is the mathematical requirement that no individual's curvature can be driven to infinity.

Practical Applications

Public Policy

Governments can use curvature minimization as a guiding principle for resource allocation, healthcare policy, and social programs, aiming to create the maximum possible well-being for the entire population.

Effective Altruism

The principles of utilitarian calculus are the mathematical foundation for the effective altruism movement, which seeks to use evidence and reason to find the most effective ways to improve the world.

AI Ethics

For an artificial intelligence, utilitarian calculus provides a clear, unambiguous objective function: act to minimize the total suffering (positive curvature) in the world. This is a powerful and safe framework for developing benevolent AI.
