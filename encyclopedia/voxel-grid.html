FAQs

What is a voxel?

A voxel is a three-dimensional unit of space, analogous to a pixel in two dimensions. It represents the smallest distinguishable box in a voxel grid.

Why is the recognition length important?

The recognition length determines how closely voxels can be packed together while still allowing for clear recognition. If the voxels are too close, recognition may become ambiguous.
