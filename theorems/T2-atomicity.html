Theorem 2: Atomicity & Countability

Exactly one unit posting per tick; concurrency at one tick is impossible

Statement

Each tick posts exactly one elementary ledger entry of size δ. Two distinct postings in the same tick violate double‑entry granularity or conservation.

Sketch

  • Assume two postings at one tick; either merge as one (violating granularity) or create unmatched credit/debit (violating conservation).
  • Hence updates are discrete and countable, establishing the unit step structure used by later results.

Implications

  • Defines the indivisible time step used in T7 (minimal period).
  • Supports integer split‑count in T5 (k = 1 and φ).
