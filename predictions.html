How is this calculated?

The Fine-structure constant, often called alpha (α), describes the strength of the interaction between light and matter. In Recognition Science, its value is not a random number but is built from the fundamental geometry of reality. The framework predicts the inverse of alpha, α-1.

What do the parts of the formula mean?

  • Geometric Seed (4π × 11): This is the starting point. It represents the total number of possible states for a recognition event in 3+1 dimensional spacetime (11) multiplied by the geometric factor for a sphere (4π). It's the "blank canvas" for the interaction.

  • Curvature Correction (δκ): The fundamental "pixels" of space (voxels) are not perfectly flat. This term corrects for their tiny, inherent curvature, which also slightly modifies the interaction strength.

By combining these three logically-derived, parameter-free components, the framework calculates a value for the fine-structure constant that matches the experimentally measured value with extraordinary precision.
