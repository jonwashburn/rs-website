FOUNDATION 2: DUAL BALANCE

∀ A, ∃ J: A→A, J²=id ≠id (debit-credit)

Plain English Explanation

Every recognition creates a 'debit' and 'credit'—like double-entry bookkeeping. This balance ensures reality doesn't 'overdraw.' It derives from discrete time: each tick needs an opposite to maintain equilibrium.

Lean Code: Entry Types

inductive Entry
  | debit : Entry
  | credit : Entry

Explanation: Recognition entries are either debits or credits—fundamental opposites.

Lean Theorem: Balance Preservation

theorem balance_invariant (ledger : LedgerState) (trans : BalancedTransaction) :
  (record_transaction ledger trans).debits = (record_transaction ledger trans).credits := by
  exact (record_transaction ledger trans).balanced

Explanation: Any transaction keeps the ledger balanced—debits always equal credits.
