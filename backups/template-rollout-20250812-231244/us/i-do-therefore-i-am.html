I Do, Therefore I Am

From identity‑as‑function to identity‑as‑recognition.

The old contract

For centuries, identity rode on function. You were what you could do: your trade, output, usefulness. That contract made sense in a world where doing was scarce and difficult.

What’s changing

As tools eat function, “I do, therefore I am” collapses. If machines can do almost everything, what’s left of us? A lot—if we ground identity in recognition, not production.

The new center: recognition

  • Witness: attention has value. Seeing truly reduces harm.
  • Discern: choose low‑curvature actions; leave people lighter.
  • Repair: when you bend something, make it right fast.
  • Rhythm: live by cadences that keep you clear and kind.

Practices to shift the center

  • Daily two‑minute listen; one tiny act of service; one repair if needed.
  • Weekly coherence block (walk, worship, shared meal, or silence).
  • Monthly simplification: remove one obligation that adds more strain than it gives.
