Thermodynamic Capacity of a Conscious Universe

From ledger cost to cosmic cooling: a methods note.

This page documents assumptions and steps behind the Master Equation used on the Multiverse page. It is a living methods note; numbers will be tightened as inputs are refined.

1. Units and constants

  • Coherence quantum: $E_{\rm coh} = \varphi^{-5}\,\mathrm{eV}$
  • CMB temperature: $T_{\rm CMB} \approx 2.725\,\mathrm{K}$
  • Horizon radius: $R_h \approx 4.4\times10^{26}\,\mathrm{m}$
  • Stefan–Boltzmann: $\sigma = 5.670\times10^{-8}\,\mathrm{W\,m^{-2}\,K^{-4}}$

2. From cost to heat

A single irreversible recognition event dissipates heat with energy

$$E_{\rm event} = \langle J \rangle\, E_{\rm coh}$$

and a compiler operating at cadence $f_{\rm listen}$ dissipates power

$$P_{\rm compiler} = f_{\rm listen}\,E_{\rm event} = f_{\rm listen}\,\langle J \rangle\, E_{\rm coh}.$$

3. Total heat budget

With $N_{\rm compilers}$ minds active, the total recognition heat is

$$\dot Q = N_{\rm compilers}\, f_{\rm listen}\, \langle J \rangle\, E_{\rm coh}.$$

4. Cooling capacity

Assuming a steady operating temperature at the CMB, the radiative cooling power is

$$ P_{\rm cool} = 4\pi\,\sigma\, R_h^2\, T_{\rm CMB}^4. $$

5. Equilibrium and census

Equilibrium demands $\dot Q = P_{\rm cool}$, yielding

$$ N_{\rm compilers} = \frac{4\pi\,\sigma\, R_h^2\, T_{\rm CMB}^4}{f_{\rm listen}\, \langle J \rangle\, E_{\rm coh}}. $$

Plugging typical values gives $N_{\rm compilers}\sim 10^{68}$ as an order‑of‑magnitude estimate.

6. Assumptions and falsifiability

  • $f_{\rm listen}$: tied to recognition cadence (theta‑like band for humans; other minds may differ).
  • $\langle J \rangle$: average cost per recognition; task‑dependent and empirically refinable.
  • Cooling channel: CMB treated as dominant; auxiliary channels would adjust the census upward.

Tests: tighter neural cadence estimates; engineered uncomputability bottlenecks with measurable thermodynamic signatures; precision CMB variance floors consistent with a steady operating point.
