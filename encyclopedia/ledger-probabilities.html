How It Works

Ledger probabilities operate on the principle of dual-balance, where each recognition event is associated with a cost. The cost function, defined as J(x) = 1/2 * (x + 1/x), ensures that the probabilities reflect the balance between different outcomes. When an event is recorded in the ledger, its associated cost influences its probability, allowing for a dynamic and responsive system that adapts to new information.

Key Properties

  • Dual-Balance: Each event has a corresponding positive and negative entry, ensuring that the ledger remains balanced.
  • Cost Efficiency: The cost function minimizes the total cost of recognition events, promoting the most probable outcomes.
